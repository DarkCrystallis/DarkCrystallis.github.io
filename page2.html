Adoption

Adoption Process

  1. Initial Inquiry: Prospective adopters can reach out to The Cat's Meow Sanctuary via phone, email, or in person to express their interest in adopting a cat.
  2. Application Submission: The sanctuary provides an adoption application form that prospective adopters need to fill out. This form collects information about the adopter's living situation, experience with pets, and preferences for the type of cat they are looking to adopt.
  3. Review and Screening: Once the adoption application is submitted, the sanctuary's adoption team reviews the application carefully. They may conduct background checks and contact references provided by the applicant.
  4. Meet and Greet: If the application meets the sanctuary's criteria, the prospective adopter is invited to visit the sanctuary for a meet and greet session with the available cats. This allows the adopter to interact with different cats and find the one that best fits their lifestyle and preferences.
  5. Home Visit: In some cases, the sanctuary may conduct a home visit to ensure that the adopter's living environment is suitable for a cat. This includes checking for potential hazards and ensuring that the home is safe and cat-friendly.
  6. Adoption Counseling: The sanctuary provides adoption counseling to help prospective adopters make informed decisions. This includes discussing the responsibilities of pet ownership, the specific needs of the cat being adopted, and addressing any concerns or questions the adopter may have.
  7. Adoption Agreement: If both the adopter and the sanctuary are satisfied with the match, an adoption agreement is signed. This agreement outlines the terms and conditions of the adoption, including spaying/neutering, vaccinations, and future care of the cat.
  8. Adoption Fee: A nominal adoption fee is typically charged to cover the cost of vaccinations, spaying/neutering, and other medical expenses incurred by the sanctuary. This fee may vary depending on the age and health condition of the cat.
  9. Finalization and Follow-Up: Once the adoption process is complete, the adopter takes their new feline friend home. The sanctuary follows up with the adopter to ensure a smooth transition and provide ongoing support and guidance as needed.
  10. Post-Adoption Support: The Cat's Meow Sanctuary remains available to provide support and assistance to adopters even after the adoption process is complete. Adopters are encouraged to reach out to the sanctuary with any questions, concerns, or updates about their adopted cat.
